<p><p>Through-hole technology is a method of mounting electronic components, primarily those that require enhanced durability. The wirewound type features resistors made from wire coiled around a core, offering high precision and stability, often used in high-power applications. In contrast, the axial type presents components with leads that extend from both ends, allowing easy insertion into PCB holes. This design is favored for its space efficiency and simplicity in circuit layouts, making it popular in various consumer and industrial electronics.</p></p>
