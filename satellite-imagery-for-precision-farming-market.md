<p><ul><li>Optical Imagery</li><li>Radar Imagery</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Satellite imagery for precision farming encompasses two main market types: optical imagery and radar imagery. Optical imagery captures visible and near-infrared light, providing detailed insights into crop health, soil conditions, and vegetation indices. It aids in monitoring growth and identifying issues like pests or diseases. In contrast, radar imagery uses synthetic aperture radar to penetrate clouds and operate under various weather conditions, offering valuable data on soil moisture, topography, and crop structure, enhancing decision-making for farmers.</p></p>
