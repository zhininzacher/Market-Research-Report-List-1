<p><p>The telemetric devices market is divided into two primary types: wired and wireless. Wired telemetric devices use physical connections, such as cables or fiber optics, to transmit data, offering stable and high-speed communication. They are typically used in environments where reliability is critical. In contrast, wireless telemetric devices employ radio frequency, satellite, or cellular networks for data transmission, allowing for greater mobility and ease of installation. This flexibility makes them ideal for remote monitoring applications and real-time data collection.</p></p>
