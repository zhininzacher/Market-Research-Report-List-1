<p><p>Interactive Voice Response (IVR) is a technology that allows callers to interact with a computer system through voice or keypad inputs. The market can be divided into two main types: speech-based IVR and touch-tone based IVR. Speech-based IVR enables users to speak their responses, providing a more natural interaction and greater flexibility. In contrast, touch-tone based IVR relies on users pressing keys on their phone keypad, which is simpler but may offer limited navigation options compared to speech recognition.</p></p>
